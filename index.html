There is only one species of alpacas, but there exist two breeds. The two breeds of alpacas are called Huacaya and Suri, which have one main difference - their fiber. The Huacaya typically looks like a teddy bear with short and curly fiber. The Suri, on the other hand, has silky fiber with locks that look like pencils. Most alpacas that exist are of the Huacaya breed - 82% to be precise. Other than the difference in fiber, both breeds have an average life span of 20 years and have soft-padded feet.
